Plants are fractal beings; infinite at all scales. What does it sound like to listen to their leaves and wind-song — to finally begin to understand them...? XÉN is the nexus of computational design and living landscapes (ecologies) — roots and recursion, leaves and lexicons, merging procedurally and parametrically. It extracts the generative grammar of living systems and exposes it as a computational palette — a few iterations, a few parameter knobs, and form emerges. Mirror it about the soil line, reverse the gravity vector, and you have the rhizosphere.

Epiphytes & Air Plants soil-decoupled · canopy-dwellers
Epiphytes drop the soil constraint entirely. They live on bark, on branches, on power lines — drawing moisture from air, nutrients from rain. The algorithm changes: no taproot, no fibrous mat. Holdfasts replace anchors. Aerial roots replace water-seeking laterals. The whole rhizospheric subroutine is bypassed.
